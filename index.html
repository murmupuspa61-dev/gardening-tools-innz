**1. Gardening Stuff**

Basically, you can group gardening tools by what they do: getting the soil ready, planting, keeping things up, harvesting, and staying safe.

**A. Getting the Soil Ready**

*   ***Small Hand Tools***

    *   **Trowel**: It's like a mini-shovel. Great for digging little holes, moving plants, and mixing up dirt.
    *   **Hand fork**: Good for loosening dirt, pulling out weeds, and letting air get to the soil in small spots.
    *   **Garden knife**: A do-it-all tool for digging, cutting roots, and splitting up plants.

*   ***Big Digging Tools***

    *   **Spade**: Has a flat, straight blade. Use it for digging and cleaning up edges.
    *   **Shovel**: The blade is curved. Use it for moving dirt, compost, or that mulch stuff.
    *   **Hoe**: Chops at weeds and gets soil loose, also good for making rows for planting.
    *   **Mattock**: Use this when the ground is super hard or rocky.

*   ***Rakes***

    *   **Leaf rake**: For leaves and light stuff.
    *   **Garden rake**: Smooths out the dirt, spreads mulch, and picks up rocks.

**B. Planting Tools**

*   **Bulb planter**: Makes even holes for bulbs and baby plants.
*   **Seed dibber**: Pokes holes for planting seeds.
*   **Transplanting trowel**: Like a small trowel but with lines to measure how deep you're planting.
*   **Gardening gloves**: Keep your hands safe from cuts, blisters, and stuff in the dirt.

**C. Pruning and Cutting**

*   **Pruning shears**: For little branches.
*   **Loppers**: Like pruning shears, but for bigger branches.
*   **Hedge shears**: Shape up those hedges and bushes.
*   **Saw**: For cutting thick branches.
*    **Weeders**: Pull out the whole weed, root and all.

**D. Keeping Things Up**

*   **Wheelbarrow**: Haul dirt, compost, plants, whatever.
*   **Garden fork**: Turns the soil, pokes holes to get air in, and breaks up lumps.
*   **Soil knife**: Chops roots, opens bags, cuts sod.
*   **Kneeling pad**: Makes it easier on your knees when you're planting or weeding.
*   **Twine**: Helps plants grow upright.
*   **Mulching tools**: Spreads mulch around nice and even.

**E. Harvesting**

*   **Harvesting knife**: Cuts stuff like veggies, herbs, and grains.
*   **Fruit picker**: Grabs fruit that's high up.
*   **Pruning shears**: Good for fruit bushes and small trees.

**2. Watering How-Tos**

Water is super important for plants. You can water by hand or set up something automatic.

**A. Watering by Hand**

*   **Watering can**: Light and easy to carry. Good for small spaces.
*   **Hosepipe**: A long hose for bigger yards.
*   **Spray nozzle**: Change the water pattern for different needs.
*   **Bucket**: The old-fashioned way to water. Still works for small gardens.

**B. Drip Systems**

*   **Drip lines**: Slowly drips water right where the plant needs it.
*   **Drippers**: Control how much water each plant gets.
*   **Pressure thingy**: Keeps the water flow even.
*   **Filters**: Keeps the drippers from getting clogged.
*   **Timer**: Turns the water on and off automatically.

    *   *Why it's cool:* Saves water, less weeds, great for veggies.

**C. Sprinklers**

*   **Spinny sprinklers**: Covers a lot of ground.
*   **Regular sprinklers**: Stays in one spot.
*   **Timers**: Turns the water on and off.
*   **Hose connectors**: Keep the water flowing right.

    *   *Why it's cool:* Easy way to water big lawns.

    *   *Not so cool:* Wastes water.

**D. Soaker Hoses**

*   Hoses that leak water slowly.

    *   *Good for:* Flower and veggie gardens.

**E. Storing and Moving Water**

*   **Rain barrels**: Collect rainwater.
*   **Pumps**: Pull water from wells or tanks.
*   **Pressure boosters**: Give you better water flow.
*   **Filters**: Keeps the water clean for watering plants.

**3. Soil and Plant Food**

*   **Compost bin**: Turns old food and yard waste into good dirt
*   **Soil tester**: Checks what's in your soil.
*   **Fertilizer spreader**: Puts fertilizer down evenly.
*   **Mulch**: Keeps the soil moist, cuts down on weeds, and makes the soil better.
*   **Potting mix**: Light soil that's good for plants in pots.

**4. Staying Safe and Keeping Tools Up**

*   **Gloves**: Keep your hands safe.
*   **Knee pads**: Make planting easier on your knees.
*   **Safety glasses**: Protect your eyes.
*   **Tool kit**: Has stuff for cleaning your tools.
*   **Storage**: Keeps your tools from rusting and hurting you.

**5. Fancy Gadgets**

*   **Soil sensors**: Tells you when to water.
*   **Timers**: Turns the water on and off automatically.
*   **Plant lights**: Helps plants grow inside.
*   **Weather thing**: Measures rain and sunlight to water plants right.

**Quick Tip:**

*   Get tools that feel good in your hands to avoid getting sore.
*   Washing and sharpening your tools will make them last longer.
*   Match your watering system to the size of your garden to save water.
